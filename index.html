The Action
You trap the raccoon humanely, drive it to a nice wooded area, and release it. It scurries off happily into a better life.
reality

The Action
You trap a stressed, disoriented animal, possibly separating a mother from kits. It is released into unfamiliar territory with no established food sources, shelter, or safety knowledge.

Survival
The raccoon adapts quickly. It's a wild animal — it knows what to do. Nature takes care of its own.
~100%
reality
📉
Survival
GPS studies spanning 15 years show only 18% of relocated raccoons survive long-term. Half are dead within 3 months. Up to 75% are dead within a year.

🗺️
New Territory
It finds a peaceful patch of woods, claims it as home, and lives out its days far from human conflict.
reality
⚔️
New Territory
That territory already has raccoons. Resident animals attack or drive out newcomers. Studies show relocated raccoons flee the release site within hours to days, often ending up in another human neighborhood.
✅
Your Problem
Solved. The raccoon is gone. You can move on. Problem eliminated humanely.
reality
🔄
Your Problem
Not solved. The food source, shelter, or attractant is still there. A new raccoon — often within days — moves into the vacuum. You will do this forever unless you address the actual cause.
👍
Legal Status
It's a live trap and a release. No harm done. Surely this is perfectly fine and legal.
reality
⚖️
Legal Status
In Texas, transporting a live raccoon without TPWD authorization is a Class C misdemeanor under the Rabies Control and Eradication Administrative Code. "I found it on YouTube" is not a defense.
