🚗 The Action

You trap the raccoon humanely, drive it to a nice wooded area, and release it. It scurries off happily into a better life.

vs
😰 The Action

You trap a stressed, disoriented animal — possibly a nursing mother separated from her kits. It is released into unfamiliar territory with no established food sources, shelter, or safety knowledge.

🌲 Survival

It's a wild animal — it knows what to do. Nature takes care of its own.

~100%
vs
📉 Survival

15 years of GPS studies show only 18% survive long-term. Half are dead within 3 months. Up to 75% are dead within a year.

18%
🗺️ New Territory

It finds a peaceful patch of woods, claims it as home, and lives out its days far from human conflict.

vs
⚔️ New Territory

That territory already has raccoons. Resident animals attack or drive out newcomers. Relocated raccoons flee release sites within hours — often ending up in another human neighborhood.

✅ Your Problem

Solved. The raccoon is gone. Problem eliminated humanely.

vs
🔄 Your Problem

Not solved. The food source and attractant are still there. A new raccoon moves into the vacuum — often within days. You will do this forever unless you address the actual cause.

👍 Legal Status

It's a live trap and a release. No harm done. Surely this is perfectly legal.

vs
⚖️ Legal Status

In Texas, transporting a live raccoon without TPWD authorization is a Class C misdemeanor under the Rabies Control and Eradication Administrative Code. "I found it on YouTube" is not a defense.
